The brief

After having replatformed their public-facing website in 2019, GOTAFE wanted to turn their attention to optimising and redesigning their core journeys and high-impact pages. One of the most important steps was to look at course pages and the course information journey more broadly.

Course page templates, which are essentially GOTAFE's "product" pages, were underperforming. So we were called in to investigate why, and to enhance these pages to help prospective students locate and understand the courses they were interested in.

The plan

GOTAFE's audience is made up of a hugely diverse range of people with significantly different backgrounds, needs and motivations. Their course offering is also incredibly comprehensive, covering everything from short courses to accredited courses. To add to this, their internal stakeholder mix was large, and their input integral to the success of the project.

Because of these factors and more, we knew that a project that did not include significant engagement with GOTAFE and the communities they serve would result in poorer outcomes for both. So to ensure we spent adequate time upfront exploring the environment more closely, we planned a three-stage approach:

1.0

User and business needs, problems to be solved

2.0

Appropriate solutions for the ideal user experience
